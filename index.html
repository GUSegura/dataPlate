Adapting the student meal plan to the needs of the Colombian household!

In 2022, the World Food Program (WFP) conducted a food security and nutrition assessment in Colombia. Findings from the report stated that it is critical to maintain and expand emergency assistance to meet the food security needs of the populations identified. The WFP recommended that such assistance should incorporate a nutritional lens to make sure vulnerable groups are able to access a nutritious diet. In this data visualization, we take a look at the food security of households with children with a focus on nutrition.

Scroll down to start.

7,097 Colombian households were surveyed in the Colombian Emergency Food Security and Nutrition Assessment (EFSA)

Of these, 47% were households with students

49% of the households with students relied on the school meal plan

That is, 1 out of every 5 Colombian households participated in the school meal plan.

Focusing on the food security and nutrition of this population provides us with insights into the nutritional effectiveness of the meal plans

How can the meal plan be improved?

We can consider the quality of the diet of meal plan households

The food consumption score (FCS) is an indicator of diet diversity, meal frequency, and nutritional value.

This index can be used to calculate household food consumption status.

Households status may be evaluated as acceptable, boderline, or poor.

Diets appear unbalanced regardless of FCS status.

Even in meal-plan households with 'acceptable' consumption, many vital food groups are absent.

65% of all households eat vegetables less than twice a week.

In Colombia, the average adult over 20 years old consumes only 32% of the recommended vegetable intake.

Despite 13% of children suffering from malnutrition, the degree to which children meet the recommended vegetable intake is largely missing from the literature.
